Haibin Zhao

About Me

Welcome! My name is Haibin Zhao (赵海滨), PhD Candidate at the Karlsruhe Institute of Technology (KIT) - the research university in the Helmholtz Association, under the supervision of Prof. Dr. Mehdi B. Tahoori and Prof. Michael Beigl.

My research revolves around EDA for neuromorphic computing systems and TinyML & EdgeAI. The former focuses on modeling neuromorphic circuits (through ML or genetic approaches) and enhancing them for a range of common challenges, such as reliability and practicality. The latter focuses on reducing the memory requirements and inference time of NNs through neural network quantization, network pruning, and neural architecture search. Both research directions will advance the deployment of artificial intelligent in edge scenarios.

Project

I have been involved in multiple projects, including:

  • MERAGEM (Modeling, Design, Realization and Automation of Printed Electronics and their Materials)

    MERAGEM is committed to advancing printed electronics in the digital economy. It is an interdisciplinary research involving materials science, physics, electrical engineering, and information technology. I take the leadership of the research subgroup on the design and optimization of printed analog neuromorphic computing systems from 2022 to 2024.

  • SDIL (Smart Data Innovation Lab)

    SDIL is positioned to foster enhanced collaboration between industry and research, focusing on cutting-edge domains like artificial intelligence, big data, and smart data technologies. Within this project, I lectured the tutorial of AI and PyTorch, developed a web-based automated feature engineering platform, and led ML-related projects such as training NNs with ultra-small datasets through Bayesian approach.

  • JuBot (Stay Young with Robot)

    The JuBot project aims to develop personalized assistive robotics for elderly people. I took the response to accelerate the human activity recognition (HAR), which facilitates the robot responsiveness in providing timely assistance. By proposing task-specified highly-efficient data preprocessing, compact neural architecture search, network quantization and pruning, the inference time of HAR models can be substantially reduced.
