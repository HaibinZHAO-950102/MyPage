Haibin Zhao

About Me

Welcome! My name is Haibin Zhao (赵海滨), PhD from the Karlsruhe Institute of Technology (KIT), Germany - the research university in the Helmholtz Association, advised by Prof. Michael Beigl for artificial intelligence and Prof. Dr. Mehdi B. Tahoori (IEEE fellow) for electronic design automation.

My research revolves around EDA for neuromorphic computing systems (hardware AI acceleration) as well as TinyML & EdgeAI (software AI acceleration). The former focuses on modeling neuromorphic hardware and enhancing them for a range of common challenges, such as reliability and practicality. The latter focuses on reducing the memory requirements and inference time of ML models through quantization, pruning, and architecture search. Both research directions will advance the deployment of artificial intelligent in edge scenarios.

    • Project

    I have been involved in multiple projects, including:

  • MERAGEM (Modeling, Design, Realization and Automation of Printed Electronics and their Materials)

    MERAGEM is committed to advancing printed electronics in the digital economy. It is an interdisciplinary research involving materials science, physics, electrical engineering, and information technology. I take the leadership of the research subgroup on the design and optimization of printed analog neuromorphic computing systems from 2022 to 2024.

  • SDIL (Smart Data Innovation Lab)

    SDIL is positioned to foster enhanced collaboration between industry and research, focusing on cutting-edge domains like artificial intelligence, big data, and smart data technologies. From 2022 to 2023, I lectured the tutorial of AI and PyTorch, developed a web-based automated feature engineering platform, and led ML-related projects such as training NNs with ultra-small-scale datasets through Bayesian approach.

  • JuBot (Stay Young with Robot)

    The JuBot project aims to develop personalized assistive robotics for elderly people. I am the head of the research group for accelerating human activity recognition (HAR) models since 2023, which facilitates the robot responsiveness in providing timely assistance. By proposing task-specified highly-efficient data preprocessing, compact neural architecture search, network quantization and pruning, the inference time of HAR models can be substantially reduced.
